About SOMOSPIE

SOMOSPIE is a modular SOil MOisture SPatial Inference Engine that allows address the coarse-grained resolution and spatial information gaps associated with satellite data. The modular components of SOMOSPIE consists of:
Input of available satellite data at its native spatial resolution.
Selection of a geographic region of interest.
Prediction, of missing values across the entire region of interest (i.e., gap-filling) and at finer-grained resolution.
Analysis and visualization of generated predictions.

SOMOSPIE and Cloud Platforms

SOMOSPIE supports reproducibility, explainability, and portability of results. Its new features allow users to:
Deploy container technology on cloud platforms to perform rapid data movement and achieve portability.
Collect workflow execution's record trails to enable data traceability and results explainability.

Download SOMOSPIE Datasets from Hydroshare

SOMOSPIE has been used to generate fine-grained, gap-free soil moisture maps of regions in US and the CONUS. From Hydroshare you can download these maps:
  • Monthly and weekly soil moisture predictions in 2010 at 1-km spatial resolution of the Oklahoma State and the Mid-west Region (ESA version 4.5)
  • Monthly and weekly soil moisture predictions in 2010 at 1-km spatial resolution of the Oklahoma State and the Mid-west Region (ESA version 6.1)
  • Monthly and weekly soil moisture predictions in 2008 at 100 m spatial resolution of the Delaware State (ESA version 4.5)
  • Annual soil moisture predictions from 1991 to 2018 at 15km resolution using terrain parameters and bioclimatic data (ESA version 4.5)
